Problem Statement

As digital platforms scale, users are exposed to overwhelming amounts of content and choices. Without personalization, users struggle to find relevant items, leading to reduced engagement and lower satisfaction.

While machine learning can enable personalization, poorly designed recommendation systems can feel confusing, biased, or untrustworthy to users.

The challenge lies not in building complex models, but in designing recommendation experiences that balance accuracy, explainability, and user control.

My Role

Led an independent ML product case study focused on defining personalization strategy, identifying user problems in discovery, and translating machine learning concepts into clear product decisions, user flows, and success metrics.

Approach

  • Identified user discovery and decision-friction problems
  • Defined personalization goals and success metrics
  • Analyzed how recommendation systems create user value
  • Evaluated trade-offs between accuracy, explainability, and complexity
  • Designed recommendation flows and measurement strategy

Key Insights

  • Users value relevance over variety.
    Too many options reduce confidence and slow decision-making.
  • Cold-start experience defines trust.
    Poor initial recommendations reduce long-term engagement.
  • Explainability increases adoption.
    Users feel more comfortable when they understand why something is recommended.
  • Personalization must evolve over time.
    Static recommendations fail as user preferences change.

Product Decisions & Trade-offs

  • Chose hybrid recommendation logic instead of pure ML-based ranking.
    Combined behavioral signals with basic rules to reduce cold-start risk.
  • Chose progressive personalization over immediate deep personalization.
    Avoided aggressive personalization early to prevent incorrect assumptions.
  • Chose explainable recommendations over black-box accuracy.
    Slightly lower precision was accepted in favor of higher user trust.
  • Chose engagement metrics over click-only optimization.
    Prioritized repeat usage and time-to-value rather than short-term clicks.

Outcome / Impact

Based on simulated data patterns and behavioral assumptions, the proposed recommendation strategy was expected to:

  • Improve content or product discovery efficiency
  • Increase engagement through personalized ranking
  • Reduce decision fatigue during browsing
  • Strengthen long-term retention through evolving personalization

Note: These outcomes represent projected impact based on product assumptions, not actual production metrics.

What I Learned

  • ML systems create value only when aligned with the user context
  • Better algorithms do not guarantee a better user experience
  • Trust and transparency matter as much as accuracy
  • Product managers must bridge technical feasibility with human behavior
  • Personalization is a product strategy, not just a model output
